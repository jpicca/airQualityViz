Observations

A few notes on our data.

  • Ozone levels appear to increase in most cities during warmer months. This is not surprising given the tendency for higher levels of ozone on hotter days.
  • Los Angeles, Houston, and to some extent Phoenix, appear to have the worst air quality on average across the country. Their AQI values appear to be improving, though. Better weather? Changes in regulations? More questions to ask.
  • Based on PM2.5 values, it seems as though 2020 may be seeing a decrease in pollution, which could be related to industry slowdowns from COVID19. However, there is a lot of uncertainty with this conclusion. Similar conclusions cannot be made about Ozone, since it is heavily influenced by temperature, and we only have data through April for 2020.
